Zines are a self published booklet (usually) similar to a magazine (hence the name)

I'm a part of a writing club at my university and we do a yearly anthology to try and get everyone writing and doing something together with the goal of becoming published. I really enjoy it and I've contributed a piece to it however its themed and didn't quite scratch the itch I had for a more open chance to get my stuff into the world. As I was musing over a short poem and a comic idea I'd been playing with I decided to try taking a stab at making a themeless monthly zine to get myself and others making stuff and just to have a place for the things we were proud of to go. 

As we all finished up our submissions and it was time to begin putting together the manuscript it became very clear that libreoffice draw wasn't going to cut it and I tried finding tutorials for doing it but could find none so I made my own:

{{ youtube(id="4MtyJICoSD4", autoplay=false) }}

I used [scribus](https://www.scribus.net/) and [pdf arranger](https://flathub.org/en/apps/com.github.jeromerobert.pdfarranger) as I refuse to ever touch adobe willingly and overall it was a great experience

For binding I used a long arm stapler I picked up from staples and I used the sliding paper cutter from the library to trim the margins as that makes all the difference especially for being able to flip through it  

Being able to hold our finished product was an awesome feeling and they're small and light enough I can mail them to some family members

If you wanna make a zine, this is the sign! Go make some cool stuff and get it out there! Don't let your works rot on a hard drive or dark corner of your room, get em out there and make em physical!
