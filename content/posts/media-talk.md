## BLAME!

I kept hearing Tsutomu Nihei’s name mentioned here and there and so I finally decided to check his stuff out and BLAME! seemed like the right place to start. Nihei’s prior experience with architecture really shines in his works and the megastructures of BLAME!. It's such a unique read with strong environmental storytelling, a cool medieval sci-fi and cyberpunk world, and an almost meditative experience that comes from taking in his art. I ordered in the master editions to my local book store as I wanted to be able to read it physically, and every time the next came in I made a day of picking it up and then reading it in a single sitting because once I started I would lose track of time lol

![BLAME!](/post-assets/media-talk/blame.jpg "picture of two pages from blame! with characters climbing on the massive megastructure")

## Tower Dungeon

With how impressed I was with BLAME! of course I had to check out his ongoing series Tower Dungeon and I was not disappointed. Nihei has come a long way with his character writing and I think the art style of Tower Dungeon is really cool. I’ve seen a few things do magic as science but seeing magic as a sort of scifi is really cool. I love the cast and I’m very excited to see where this goes.

## Fire Punch

Fire Punch was filled with highs and lows that always kept me on my toes. Its certainly [dead dove](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/dead-dove-do-not-eat) so make sure you know what you’re getting into, but it has fascinating commentary on human nature and morality as well as striking artwork and characters that stick with you.

![agni](/post-assets/media-talk/agni.jpg "picture of agni from fire punch forcing a smile")


## Nimona (Comic)

I had seen the movie ages ago and I loved it, so when I was wandering through my local bookstore and saw the comic I had to pick it up. It was a really enjoyable read and I love the art style. It’s quite different from the movie so give it a go!

## Vampire: The Masquerade - The Complete Series (Comic)

I’ve gotten into playing Vampire: The Masquerade and one day my girlfriend went looking for more VTM stuff to read between sessions and she stumbled upon the comic run. It was sold out everywhere and it seemed the print run was done and gone. We couldn’t find a way to read it so on a whim I shot an email to the author and he actually got back to us! We were able to work something out and he mailed over a spare copy. We were both super excited when we got the chance to read it (she got to read it first) and it was exactly what we were looking for. Drama, infighting, hunters, even a werewolf or two, it has it all. Good vampire media can be hard to find and it scratched all the itches we were hoping it would. I really hope it gets another print run because it really deserves it.

## Berserk

I didn’t really know what I was getting into with Berserk but once I started getting into the golden age arc I was hooked. It has some dated portrayals of certain things but overall it's a masterpiece filled with medieval combat, political intrigue, astonishing artwork, and lovable characters (except Puck, I hate Puck with a passion). I’m quite sad that the author passed and I’ll likely stop before getting into an unfinished arc. 

## Serial Experiments Lain

I saw Lain a few years ago but it's as iconic as ever. Lain is a cyberpunk story focusing on “the wired” and a girl named Lain’s unique connection to it. I don’t want to say too much and risk spoiling it but it has some interesting philosophy and it's worth your time

## Takopi’s Original Sin

Heed the warnings on this one as it's also [dead dove](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/dead-dove-do-not-eat), but oh my glob is it incredible. This is quite possibly my favorite show of 2025. Watching Takopi’s Original Sin is like shoving a red hot rod of metal into your brain and seeing what sizzles. There’s a little something in there for everyone to get hit right in the feels

![takopis](/post-assets/media-talk/takopis.jpg "picture of Naoki Azuma from the anime adaptation of takopi's origional sin having a panic attack")


## Call of the Night

I loved watching and reading this one. It's a neat blend of vampires and slice of life that has queer themes and some mental health stuff. It really hit for me, but it seems most people I’ve gotten to watch it didn’t really like it in the beginning and had little desire to keep going. It's not for everyone and I guess it isn’t “peak fiction” but I personally loved it.
